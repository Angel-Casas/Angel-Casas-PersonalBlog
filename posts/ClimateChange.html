A light in the dark

The Scientific Method, which is a method by which science is carried out, involves building hypothesis based on previous observations about a phenomenon (induction), the study of the implications of this hypothesis (deduction)and the experimental testing of this hypothesis which must be verifiable and repeatable/replicable by anyone. This method allows for predictions to appear as a consequence of studying a subject and the successive discarding of erroneus or invalid information. It is this predictions which can then be tested for it's ability to explain the phenomenon studied. If we can successfully predict an event from a given initial hypothesis through experiment we say we have a new scientific truth. Having cleared this concept out we must remember the science of climate change is of a very recent origin and the modern collection of data with respect to global temperatures only dates from as late as the 1880's, although there are many accurate temperature recordings since the invention of the modern thermometer in the early 1700s which have not been digitalized. Thus, whenever you hear we have broken a new temperature record remember, this is true only with respect to the last 100 years of available modern data, since monitoring global surface temperatures was not an easy feat until modern times.
